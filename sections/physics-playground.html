Experimental Physics Engine

This playground demonstrates the Rapier physics engine integrated with Three.js for realistic 3D physics simulations. Experiment with different object types and observe how they interact with gravity, friction, and each other.

The physics engine calculates realistic collision responses, momentum transfer, and energy conservation. This environment is designed to help visualize and understand the principles of rigid body dynamics.

Use the controls in the upper right corner to add objects to the scene. You can switch between "Tactile Mode" for direct manipulation and "Transform Mode" for precise positioning of objects.

Physics Principles

The simulation demonstrates several key physics principles:

  • Gravity: Objects fall due to simulated gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/sÂ²).
  • Friction: Objects sliding on surfaces gradually slow down due to friction.
  • Collisions: When objects collide, momentum and energy are transferred based on their mass and velocity.
  • Restitution: The "bounciness" of objects is determined by their material properties.
  • Mass and Inertia: Heavier objects require more force to move and resist changes in motion.

Try creating different arrangements of objects and observe how they interact. The physics engine handles all calculations in real-time, providing an accurate representation of how these objects would behave in the real world.
