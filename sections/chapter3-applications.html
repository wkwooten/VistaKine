3.3 Applications: Torque and Angular Momentum

Exploring physical applications of the cross product.

Torque

Torque is the rotational analog of force, calculated as the cross product of position and force:

\( \vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F} \)

where \( \vec{r} \) is the position vector from the pivot point to where the force is applied.

In the visualization, see how changing the force direction or the point of application affects the resulting torque.

Angular Momentum

Angular momentum is calculated as the cross product of position and linear momentum:

\( \vec{L} = \vec{r} \times \vec{p} \)

Angular momentum is conserved in a closed system, which explains why objects rotate faster when their mass distribution gets closer to the axis of rotation.
