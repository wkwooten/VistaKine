3.4 Triple Products and Volume

Scalar Triple Product

The scalar triple product is calculated as the dot product of one vector with the cross product of two others:

\( \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \)

This equals the volume of the parallelepiped formed by the three vectors.

In the visualization, drag the vectors to see how they form a three-dimensional shape and how the volume changes.

Vector Triple Product

The vector triple product is the cross product of a vector with the cross product of two other vectors:

\( \vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \)

This can be rewritten using the BAC-CAB formula:

\( \vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) = \vec{b}(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{c}) - \vec{c}(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}) \)
