2.3 Dot Products

Understanding the dot product operation between vectors.

Calculating Dot Products

The dot product between two vectors \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \) is defined as:

\( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_x b_x + a_y b_y + a_z b_z \)

Geometrically, it can also be calculated as:

\( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta \)

where \( \theta \) is the angle between the vectors.

In the visualization, notice how the dot product changes as the angle between vectors changes. When vectors are perpendicular, their dot product is zero.

Properties of the Dot Product

The dot product has several important properties:

  • It results in a scalar (not a vector)
  • It is commutative: \( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \vec{b} \cdot \vec{a} \)
  • Perpendicular vectors have a dot product of zero
  • Parallel vectors have a dot product equal to the product of their magnitudes
  • The dot product of a vector with itself gives the square of its magnitude: \( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{a} = |\vec{a}|^2 \)
