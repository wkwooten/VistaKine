3.2 Geometric Interpretation

Area of a Parallelogram

The magnitude of the cross product \( |\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| \) equals the area of the parallelogram formed by vectors \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \):

\( |\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta \)

where \( \theta \) is the angle between the vectors.

In the visualization, change the vectors to see how the area of the parallelogram changes. Notice that when the vectors are parallel, the area becomes zero.

Properties of the Cross Product

The cross product has several important properties:

  • It results in a vector (not a scalar)
  • It is anti-commutative: \( \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = -(\vec{b} \times \vec{a}) \)
  • Parallel vectors have a cross product of zero
  • The magnitude is maximum when vectors are perpendicular
  • The cross product of a vector with itself is zero: \( \vec{a} \times \vec{a} = \vec{0} \)
