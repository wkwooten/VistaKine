Chapter 1: Understanding 3D Space

Explore three-dimensional space and learn how to describe positions, orientations, and transformations mathematically.

Understanding three-dimensional space is fundamental to physics, engineering, computer graphics, and many other fields. In this chapter, we'll develop the mathematical tools needed to work in 3D, with a focus on visualization and intuition.

Learning Objectives

  • Navigate and manipulate objects in 3D coordinate systems
  • Convert between different coordinate representations
  • Understand matrices as transformations in space
  • Apply matrix operations to transform objects in 3D

1.1 Introduction to 3D Coordinates

Learn how to specify positions in three-dimensional space and navigate the coordinate system.

1.2 Coordinate Systems

Explore different ways to represent positions in space, including Cartesian, spherical, and cylindrical coordinates.

1.3 Matrices as Transformations

Discover how matrices can represent rotations, scaling, and other transformations in 3D space.

1.4 Interactive Matrix Lab

Experiment with different matrix operations and see their effects on 3D objects in real time.

Chapter Summary

In this chapter, you'll build a solid foundation for understanding 3D space, which is essential for the vector operations we'll explore in Chapter 2. The interactive visualizations will help you develop an intuitive grasp of how coordinates and transformations work in three dimensions.
