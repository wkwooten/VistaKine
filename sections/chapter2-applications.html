2.4 Applications: Force and Displacement

Work: Force Ã— Displacement

In physics, work is calculated as the dot product of force and displacement vectors:

\( W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = |F||d|\cos\theta \)

This means that only the component of force in the direction of displacement contributes to work.

In the visualization, change the angle between the force and displacement vectors to see how the work changes. When they're perpendicular, no work is done!

Projection

The dot product allows us to find the projection of one vector onto another:

\( \text{proj}_{\vec{b}}\vec{a} = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|} \)

This is useful in many physics applications, including finding components of vectors in specific directions.
