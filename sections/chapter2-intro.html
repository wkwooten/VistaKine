2.1 What are Vectors?

Vector Definition

Vectors are mathematical objects that have both magnitude (size) and direction.

In contrast, scalars (like temperature or mass) only have magnitude.

The visualization above shows vectors in 3D space. Notice how each vector has both a length (magnitude) and points in a specific direction.

Vector Representation

In 3D Cartesian coordinates, a vector is represented by three components:

\( \vec{v} = (v_x, v_y, v_z) \)

These components represent the projection of the vector onto each coordinate axis.
