Cross Products

Understand the cross product operation between vectors and its applications in physics.

What is the Cross Product?

The cross product is a binary operation on two vectors in three-dimensional space that results in a vector that is perpendicular to both of the vectors being multiplied.

For two vectors a and b, the cross product a × b is defined as:

|a × b| = |a| |b| sin(θ)

where θ is the angle between the vectors.

Properties of Cross Products

  • Anti-commutativity: a × b = -(b × a)
  • Distributivity: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c
  • Scalar multiplication: (ka) × b = k(a × b) = a × (kb)
  • Perpendicularity: a × b is perpendicular to both a and b

Applications in Physics

The cross product has numerous applications in physics, including:

Torque

Torque is calculated as the cross product of the position vector and the force vector: τ = r × F

Angular Momentum

Angular momentum is calculated as the cross product of the position vector and the linear momentum: L = r × p

Magnetic Force

The magnetic force on a moving charged particle is calculated as: F = q(v × B)
