Understanding Cross Products

The cross product of two vectors produces a third vector that is perpendicular to both of the original vectors. The magnitude of this new vector equals the area of the parallelogram formed by the two original vectors.

Key Properties:

  • The cross product vector is perpendicular to both input vectors
  • The direction follows the right-hand rule
  • The magnitude equals the area of the parallelogram
  • Cross products are anti-commutative: A × B = -(B × A)

Mathematical Formula:

A × B = |A||B|sin(θ)n̂

Where θ is the angle between vectors and n̂ is the unit vector perpendicular to the plane containing A and B.

A × B = (AyBz - AzBy, AzBx - AxBz, AxBy - AyBx)
